# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-27*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Premium Japanese steel kitchen knives designed in the UK for home cooks who care about craft.
**What it does:** Shimeru Knives sells a curated range of Japanese-style chef knives (Gyuto, Santoku, Nakiri, Kiritsuke, Bunka, Bread, Cleaver, Butcher) and knife sets, priced £45–80. Every knife ships in a presentation gift box with free UK shipping.
**Product category:** Japanese kitchen knives / chef knives
**Product type:** E-commerce (DTC)
**Business model:** Direct-to-consumer online retail. Free UK shipping on all orders. Payment via Stripe with Klarna/Clearpay BNPL options.

## Target Audience
**Target customers:** UK home cooks aged 25–55 who cook regularly and want better tools than supermarket knives, but aren't looking for £200+ artisan blades. Also gift buyers (birthdays, housewarmings, weddings, Christmas).
**Decision-makers:** The person cooking at home; gift buyers choosing for a partner, parent, or friend who cooks.
**Primary use case:** Replacing dull, heavy Western kitchen knives with sharper, lighter, better-performing Japanese steel.
**Jobs to be done:**
- "I want a knife that actually cuts properly — my current knives are useless"
- "I want to buy a quality knife as a gift that looks impressive"
- "I want to level up my cooking without spending £200+ on a single knife"
**Use cases:**
- Daily home cooking (vegetables, meat, fish)
- First "proper knife" purchase for someone getting into cooking
- Premium gift in presentation packaging
- Replacing a worn-out knife set

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Most home cooks use dull, heavy Western knives that make food prep slow and frustrating. They know better knives exist but the Japanese knife market feels intimidating, expensive, and full of dropshippers selling rebranded Chinese imports.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Supermarket/budget knives: Dull quickly (54–58 HRC), heavy, poor edge retention
- Premium Western knives (Wusthof, Zwilling): Better quality but thicker edge angles (20–25°), heavier, higher price
- Amazon/dropship "Japanese" knives: Poor quality, no UK support, returns go to China, inconsistent steel
- Artisan Japanese knives (£150–400+): Intimidating, high maintenance, overkill for most home cooks
**What it costs them:** Frustrating prep time, crushed rather than sliced food, wasted money on knives that go dull in weeks.
**Emotional tension:** "I don't know what I'm doing — I don't want to waste money on the wrong knife or something I can't maintain."

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Amazon/eBay dropshippers selling "Damascus" knives — fall short because poor quality control, no UK support, misleading marketing, returns require shipping to China.
**Secondary:** Western premium brands (Wusthof, Zwilling, Robert Welch) — fall short because thicker edge angles, heavier blades, less sharp out of the box, higher price points for comparable quality.
**Indirect:** Supermarket knife sets (ProCook, Tefal) — fall short because they go dull quickly, cheap steel, not designed for precision work.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- 15° edge angle (vs 20–25° Western) — measurably sharper cuts
- 60+ HRC hardness — holds edge 2–3x longer than standard kitchen knives
- 67-layer Damascus steel options — visual beauty plus structural strength
- UK-based with real customer service and UK return address (explicit anti-dropshipper positioning)
- Every knife ships in a presentation gift box at no extra charge
- Knife Guide quiz helps beginners choose the right knife
**How we do it differently:** Combines Japanese steel performance with UK brand trust, accessibility, and education. We demystify Japanese knives for the everyday cook.
**Why that's better:** You get professional-grade sharpness without the intimidation, maintenance anxiety, or risk of buying from an anonymous overseas seller.
**Why customers choose us:** Quality + trust + price point. Better than budget knives, more accessible than artisan, and backed by a real UK business.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Japanese knives are fragile / high maintenance" | Our knives are double-bevel and designed for home cooks. Care is simple: hand wash, dry, store properly. They hold their edge far longer than Western knives so you sharpen less often. |
| "I can get a Damascus knife on Amazon for £20" | Those are typically dropshipped from China with inconsistent steel and no UK support. We're a UK company shipping branded knives from the UK with a real return address. |
| "I don't know which knife to get" | Our Knife Guide quiz recommends the right knife based on what you cook, your experience level, and what matters to you. |

**Anti-persona:** Professional chefs who need specific single-bevel Japanese knives (Yanagiba, Deba). Knife collectors looking for artisan/hand-forged pieces. People who just want the cheapest option.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Current knives are dull, heavy, and frustrating. Food gets crushed instead of sliced. They've wasted money on cheap knives that don't last.
**Pull:** Promise of noticeably sharper cuts, lighter weight, beautiful design, gift-ready packaging, and a UK brand they can trust.
**Habit:** "My current knives are fine" / familiarity with Western knife styles / inertia of not knowing which Japanese knife to choose.
**Anxiety:** "Will I ruin it? Is it hard to maintain? What if I pick the wrong one? Is this just another overpriced dropship knife?"
